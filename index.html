The basics

What exactly is a chatbot?

Strip away the buzzwords and a chatbot is just a program with one job: take a message in, and send a sensible reply back. How it decides that reply is where things get interesting.

Definition

A conversational interface

Instead of clicking menus or filling forms, you type — or speak — in plain language, and the system responds the same way. It sits in front of a website, an app, or a phone line.

Why it matters

Always-on support

A chatbot doesn't sleep, doesn't get tired of the same question, and can talk to thousands of people at once — which is why they've become the front line for customer support, learning, and booking.

Under the hood

The path from your message to its reply

Every message a chatbot receives moves through the same four stages, in this order, before an answer is sent back.

01

Input capture

Your typed or spoken message is collected and cleaned — punctuation, typos, and filler words are handled first.

02

Understanding (NLP)

Natural Language Processing breaks the sentence into meaning: what's the intent, and what details (dates, names, items) matter?

03

Deciding a response

A rule engine or a trained model picks the best reply — either from a fixed set of answers, or generated fresh, word by word.

04

Reply delivery

The response is formatted and sent back in under a second, and the conversation's context is saved for the next message.

Not all chatbots are equal

Rule-based vs. AI-based

The word "chatbot" covers two very different kinds of systems — one follows a script, the other learns patterns from data.

🧩 Rule-based

  • Follows fixed if-this-then-that logic
  • Only understands phrases it's programmed for
  • Cheap to build, easy to predict
  • Breaks on anything unexpected
  • Good fit: FAQs, order status, simple menus

🧠 AI-based

  • Trained on large volumes of real conversation
  • Understands intent, not just keywords
  • Improves as it sees more data
  • Needs more computing power to run
  • Good fit: tutoring, open Q&A, assistants
